Our Team - Who We Are

Our Board of Directors

Rochelle Keogh, Ed.D.

With nearly three decades of experience in international education, Rochelle has worked with thousands of educators worldwide, helping transform community-driven ideas into impactful initiatives. Her vision led her to found LifeThought International, connecting grassroots leaders with emerging professionals to foster community change. Rochelle holds a Doctorate in Higher Education Teaching, and a Master’s degree in Teaching English to Speakers of Other Languages. She has developed teacher training workshops for educators in 89 countries, working with institutions like Fulbright, the U.S. Department of State, and ministries of education globally.

Simon Keogh, MBA

Simon holds an MBA focused on strategic change from Edinburgh Business School and has spent 30 years honing his strategic development skills, working with organizations from small nonprofits to global companies like IBM and Walmart. He combines a deep understanding of stakeholder needs and strategic planning to help projects achieve sustainable goals. His true passion lies in grassroots projects, where he believes strategic guidance can have the biggest community impact.

Lindsay Gassin

Lindsay graduated summa cum laude from Brigham Young University with a degree in marriage and family science. For over twenty years she has had the privilege to serve alongside thousands of youth and young adults as a youth leader and mentor, and she currently serves as president to a youth group organization over several hundred youth in northwest Arkansas. She and her family have participated in multiple medical missions to underprivileged areas all around the world. She has a passion for empowering the rising generation and helping those all around the world in need.

Our Executive Council and Rising Leaders

Leah, Director of International Education Projects

Driven by a passion for international education, Leah serves as the Director of International Education Projects. Leah studied Spanish with a focus on literature and culture, along with Latin American studies and Global Community Impact, and has 2.5 years of coursework in Elementary Education and TEFL certification. With experience teaching English to learners in four countries, Leah brings both hands-on teaching skills and a global perspective to her work. Her recent work in Tunisia has ignited her passion for mentoring young teachers.

Abigail, Director of Leadership Development

Abigail is the Director of Leadership Development, where she’s dedicated to empowering emerging leaders and developing our project volunteers. A certified teacher in the gifted and talented program at her local public school, Abigail brings a background in education together with expertise in materials development, project management, and youth leadership.

Alexander, Director of Communications

Alexander is dedicated to tackling the world’s toughest challenges through impactful cross-cultural collaboration. A dual major in Psychology and Chinese with minors in Global Business and Civic Engagement Leadership, he is also pursuing a Master’s in Professional Language (Chinese). He has led international research teams, presented his findings at several international conferences, competed in a global Chinese competition (where he was a finalist!), and directed student leadership teams at his university.

Mirabella, Director of Global Development Projects

Mirabella has a background in Public Health Promotion and Middle Eastern Studies. She brings with her experience in project management, research, academic writing, and a passion for all things international, making her perfect for her role leading our global development projects. Having served as the Student Body Director of Diversity and Inclusion at her university, competed in Model United Nations, and won several awards in national debate and mock trial competitions, Mirabella is also an excellent mentor for our project volunteers.

Our Commitment to Professional Development

With the values of continuous learning, innovation, and professional growth as essential components of our mission, we are dedicated to providing young staff members with meaningful opportunities for professional development, skill enhancement, and leadership through mentorship and real world experience. These emerging leaders serve in important leadership roles in our organization, passing on what they are learning by overseeing and mentoring project interns and volunteers.

Recently, LifeThought young leaders have served as project directors, led planning meetings, sourced international projects, and presented LifeThought projects at international conferences! They have taken courses in project management and nonprofit management. These young people are gaining confidence and learning real world skills as they collaborate to support international partners.
